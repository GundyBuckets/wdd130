The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time (1998)

Often hailed as one of the greatest video games of all time, "Ocarina of Time" follows the adventures of Link as he embarks on a quest to thwart the evil plans of Ganondorf. Known for its revolutionary 3D graphics, intricate dungeons, and time-travel mechanics, this game set a new standard for action-adventure games.

The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild (2017)

This entry reinvents the Zelda formula, offering players an expansive open world to explore, complete with breathtaking landscapes and dynamic weather. Players take on the role of Link, awakening from a century-long slumber to face the enigmatic Calamity Ganon. With an emphasis on exploration, survival, and nonlinear gameplay, "Breath of the Wild" received widespread acclaim for its freedom and immersive world.

The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past (1991)

Considered a classic of the 16-bit era, "A Link to the Past" returns players to the top-down perspective of the original Legend of Zelda. Players guide Link through a parallel world of light and dark, facing challenging puzzles and dungeons in order to rescue Princess Zelda and defeat the malevolent wizard Agahnim.

The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask (2000)

A direct sequel to "Ocarina of Time," "Majora's Mask" offers a unique and darker experience. Link finds himself in the land of Termina, facing a countdown to impending doom. With the help of a mysterious mask and the ability to manipulate time, Link must prevent the moon from crashing into the land, all while experiencing the deeply emotional stories of Termina's inhabitants.
